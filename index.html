Utilisation and practice management

Know when to fill the diary, when to recruit, and which systems need tightening.

Hive teaches utilisation as one of the clearest measures of how full each practitioner’s diary really is. Too high and the team risks burnout. Too low and there is capacity to grow. The dashboard turns that into a live management signal.

Utilisation rate Booked appointment time ÷ available billable time

Available time excludes training, breaks, meetings, administration and strategic work, so the number is fair and useful.

Above target

Practitioners may be overloaded. Time to review support, scheduling, recruitment or service mix.

Below target

The diary has room to grow. Time to focus lead generation, conversion or reactivation activity.

Find the profit gap

When a treatment underperforms, the dashboard helps diagnose why.

  • Rebooking gap: patients are seen once but not booked into the next step.
  • Pricing gap: the appointment is busy but the average value is too low.
  • Outcome gap: the treatment pathway is not completing or patients drop out.
  • Capacity gap: demand exists, but the right clinic or practitioner slots are unavailable.
  • Marketing gap: profitable services are not getting enough enquiries.
